Chromatography & Separation Instruments

üß™ High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)

  • Instruments: Agilent 1260/1290, Waters Alliance/Acquity UPLC
  • Detectors: UV-Vis (214nm, 280nm), fluorescence, RI
  • Applications: SEC (size exclusion), CEX (cation exchange), RP-HPLC
  • Columns: TSKgel G3000SWXL (SEC), ProPac WCX-10 (CEX)
  • 21 CFR Part 11: Audit trail, electronic signatures

üìä Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC-HPLC)

  • Purpose: Aggregates, fragments, monomer quantification
  • Column: TSKgel G3000SWXL (7.8 mm √ó 30 cm)
  • Mobile Phase: 200 mM phosphate, pH 6.8
  • Detection: UV 280nm
  • Specifications: >95% monomer, <5% HMWS, <1% LMWS

‚ö° Capillary Electrophoresis (CE-SDS, cIEF)

  • Instrument: Sciex PA800 Plus, Protein Simple Maurice
  • CE-SDS: Purity analysis (reducing/non-reducing)
  • cIEF: Charge variant analysis (pI determination)
  • Imaging CE: Automated, high-throughput
  • Advantages: Small sample volume, fast (30-60 min)

üî¨ Ion Exchange Chromatography (CEX, AEX)

  • CEX-HPLC: Charge variant separation (acidic, main, basic)
  • Column: ProPac WCX-10, MabPac SCX-10
  • Gradient: Salt gradient (0-500 mM NaCl)
  • AEX-HPLC: Alternative charge variant method
  • Applications: Process characterization, release testing

Mass Spectrometry & Structural Analysis

üß¨ Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (LC-MS)

  • Instruments: Thermo Orbitrap, AB Sciex TripleTOF, Waters Xevo
  • Applications: Intact mass, peptide mapping, glycan analysis
  • Ionization: ESI (electrospray ionization)
  • Resolution: High-resolution (Orbitrap >100,000)
  • Mass Accuracy: <5 ppm

üîç Peptide Mapping (LC-MS/MS)

  • Workflow: Trypsin digestion ‚Üí LC separation ‚Üí MS/MS
  • Purpose: Primary structure confirmation, PTM identification
  • Software: BioPharma Finder, Byonic, PEAKS
  • Coverage: >95% sequence coverage required
  • Modifications: Oxidation, deamidation, glycation

üå∏ Glycan Analysis (LC-MS, HILIC-FLR)

  • Release: PNGase F enzymatic digestion
  • Labeling: 2-AB (fluorescent) or native MS
  • HILIC-FLR: Waters GlycoWorks (fluorescence detection)
  • LC-MS: Profiling with mass assignment
  • Reporting: % galactosylation, fucosylation, sialylation

üì° MALDI-TOF Mass Spectrometry

  • Instrument: Bruker Autoflex, AB Sciex TOF/TOF
  • Applications: Intact mass, glycan profiling
  • Matrix: Sinapinic acid (proteins), DHB (glycans)
  • Speed: Rapid screening (<5 min/sample)
  • Mass Range: 10-300 kDa

Protein Characterization Instruments

üî¨ SDS-PAGE & Western Blot

  • System: Bio-Rad Mini-PROTEAN, Invitrogen XCell
  • Imaging: ChemiDoc, Odyssey CLx (fluorescence)
  • Purpose: Purity, molecular weight, identity
  • Conditions: Reducing (2-ME) and non-reducing
  • Western: Antibody-based detection (specificity)

üìè UV-Vis Spectrophotometer

  • Instruments: Thermo Nanodrop, Agilent Cary 60
  • Applications: Protein concentration (A280)
  • Calculation: Beer-Lambert law (extinction coefficient)
  • Micro-volume: Nanodrop (1-2 ŒºL sample)
  • QC: UV scan (260/280 ratio for DNA contamination)

üå°Ô∏è Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC)

  • Instrument: Malvern MicroCal VP-Capillary DSC
  • Purpose: Thermal stability (Tm determination)
  • Output: Melting temperature, unfolding transitions
  • Applications: Formulation development, comparability
  • Stability Indicator: Higher Tm = more stable protein

üí° Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)

  • Instrument: Malvern Zetasizer, Wyatt DynaPro
  • Purpose: Particle size, aggregation monitoring
  • Size Range: 0.3 nm - 10 Œºm
  • Output: Hydrodynamic radius (Rh), polydispersity
  • Applications: Formulation screening, stability

Bioassays & Functional Testing

‚≠ê Cell-Based Potency Assay

  • Purpose: Functional biological activity measurement
  • Example (mAb): ADCC, CDC, neutralization assay
  • Detection: Luciferase, fluorescence, cell viability
  • Plate Reader: BMG CLARIOstar, Molecular Devices
  • Acceptance: Relative potency 70-130% vs. reference

üß≤ Binding ELISA (Potency)

  • Format: Sandwich ELISA, direct binding
  • Target: Antigen binding for therapeutic antibodies
  • Detection: HRP-conjugated secondary, TMB substrate
  • Plate Reader: Thermo Multiskan, BioTek Synergy
  • Validation: Precision, accuracy, linearity, range

üî¨ Flow Cytometry

  • Instruments: BD FACSCanto, Beckman Coulter CytoFLEX
  • Applications: Cell-surface binding, ADCC assays
  • Fluorochromes: FITC, PE, APC, BV421
  • Analysis: FlowJo, FCS Express software
  • Quantitative: MFI (mean fluorescence intensity)

üì° Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR)

  • Instrument: Biacore 8K, Cytiva Biacore T200
  • Purpose: Real-time binding kinetics (kon, koff, KD)
  • Chip: CM5, Protein A/G for antibodies
  • Applications: Affinity determination, epitope binning
  • Label-Free: No fluorescent tags required

Microbiological Testing Equipment

‚úÖ Sterility Testing

  • Method: USP <71> Direct inoculation or membrane filtration
  • Media: Tryptic Soy Broth (TSB), Fluid Thioglycollate (FTM)
  • Incubation: 14 days at 20-25¬∞C (TSB) and 30-35¬∞C (FTM)
  • Automation: BACTEC FX, BacT/ALERT (rapid detection)
  • Isolators: Aseptic technique in LAF hood

ü¶† Endotoxin Testing (LAL)

  • Method: Limulus Amebocyte Lysate (LAL) kinetic chromogenic
  • Instrument: Charles River Endosafe nexgen-PTS
  • Cartridge: Pre-filled disposable cartridge
  • Limit: <1 EU/mg protein (typically 0.5 EU/mg)
  • Speed: 15-minute result vs. 3-hour gel clot

üß´ Bioburden Testing

  • Method: Membrane filtration (0.45Œºm)
  • Media: Tryptic Soy Agar (TSA) plates
  • Incubation: 3-5 days at 30-35¬∞C
  • Application: Pre-sterilization samples only
  • Limit: <10 CFU/100 mL (before sterile filtration)

üå¨Ô∏è Environmental Monitoring

  • Frequency: Continuous (particles), routine (viable)
